## Problems with a social-only presence

1. **No owned home base.** Everything depends on Meta platforms. If someone Googles the business, there's no site to land on — they must already be on Facebook. Many prospective customers (especially businesses booking group training) expect a website before they'll call.
2. **No clear services/pricing page.** Price ($60), class length (~2 hrs), what's covered (adult/child/infant CPR, AED, choking), and group/on-site options are scattered across posts, not presented cleanly in one place.
3. **Booking friction.** The only paths are Messenger or phone. There's no simple, skimmable "here's what you get → call/request a seat" flow, and no group-class request path for workplaces.
4. **Weak local SEO surface.** No indexable website means no LocalBusiness schema, no service pages, no way to rank for "CPR class Clearwater," "CPR certification near me," "group CPR training Pinellas," etc. Competitors and national online-cert mills capture that search traffic.
5. **Trust signals underused.** A genuinely elite reputation (5.0/68, firefighter-taught, 100% FB recommend) is buried inside social platforms instead of front-and-center on a page the owner controls.
6. **Photos are watermarked & social-locked.** The authentic class photos only live on Facebook at low resolution with a phone-number watermark.

# The Panel — Seven Expert Reviewers

A simulated panel assembled specifically for a **local, in-person CPR-certification business** in Clearwater whose entire presence is currently on social media. Each persona is chosen to catch a failure mode the others would miss.

---

### 1. Dana Whitfield — Owner/Operator of a thriving local CPR & first-aid training company
**Why on the panel:** She runs the exact kind of business and knows what actually drives bookings (groups, recerts, word of mouth) and what owners get embarrassed by.
**Cares most about:** Believable claims, group/corporate lead capture, pricing clarity, not over-promising certifications, "does this sound like a real instructor or a mill?"
**Notices what others miss:** When copy accidentally implies a certification body or scope the owner can't back up; missing recert / on-site angles.

### 2. Marisol Reyes — Local Clearwater customer (mom of two, also books CPR for her dental office)
**Why on the panel:** She *is* the target market twice over — a parent and a workplace decision-maker.
**Cares most about:** Can I quickly see price, what's covered, how long, and how to sign up? Will it be boring or scary? Is it near me?
**Notices what others miss:** Jargon, buried phone number, anything that feels corporate/cold instead of local and human.

### 3. Terrence Boyd — Local SEO specialist
**Why on the panel:** The business has zero website SEO surface today; this is the single biggest growth lever.
**Cares most about:** Title/meta, H1 with service + city, LocalBusiness schema, NAP consistency, indexable service content, "CPR class Clearwater" intent.
**Notices what others miss:** Schema mistakes, NAP drift, thin content, missing FAQ that matches search queries.

### 4. Priya Nair — Conversion-rate optimization (CRO) strategist
**Why on the panel:** A pretty site that doesn't convert calls is worthless to the owner.
**Cares most about:** One obvious primary action, sticky mobile call button, friction in the request flow, trust proof placement above the fold.
**Notices what others miss:** Competing CTAs, weak button labels, proof that appears too late, forms that ask too much.

### 5. Alex Chen — Mobile UX & accessibility designer
**Why on the panel:** Most local-service traffic is mobile, and CPR audiences include older adults and workplaces with accessibility needs.
**Cares most about:** Tap-target size, contrast, focus states, no horizontal scroll, readable type, keyboard/screen-reader support, reduced-motion.
**Notices what others miss:** Contrast failures on red buttons, hover-only interactions, missing labels/alt text, hero too tall on small screens.

### 6. Jordan Blake — Direct-response copywriter (local services)
**Why on the panel:** The offer is emotional (saving a life) and practical (a card in 2 hours). Copy must do both without cheese.
**Cares most about:** Concrete, specific, verifiable copy; a headline that answers what/why-trust/next-step; avoiding generic "quality you can trust" filler.
**Notices what others miss:** Vague claims, missing urgency that's honest, weak subheads, testimonial selection.

### 7. Sam Rivera — Performance engineer
**Why on the panel:** Speed is a ranking and conversion factor, and the deliverable must stay pure static HTML/CSS/JS.
**Cares most about:** LCP, image weight, render-blocking resources, no heavy frameworks/fonts, CLS from images, minimal JS.
**Notices what others miss:** Oversized hero images, layout shift from unsized media, unnecessary web fonts, blocking scripts.
